# Build: Knights of the Toxic God - Infinite Tech Scaling

## Short Description
An exponentially scaling build that uses the Knights of the Toxic God origin to achieve massive technology output (e.g., 35,000+ tech by year 57). The strategy abuses the infinite scaling of Squire jobs, which buff the output of Knight jobs. By stacking bonuses that apply to both politicians and scientists onto the Knights, their research output can grow without limit.

## Detailed Strategy

### Core Mechanic (The Exploit)
The build revolves around the interaction between two special jobs from the Knights of the Toxic God origin: Knights and Squires. 
- **Knights** consume alloys to produce research, unity, and naval capacity.
- **Squires** provide amenities and, most importantly, buff the efficiency of Knights.

In the current patch, Squire jobs are **infinite**. By reducing housing usage to -100% through various modifiers, you can stack an unlimited number of Squires on your starting habitat. Furthermore, Knights benefit from **all job bonuses that affect politicians and scientists**, allowing for incredible bonus stacking.

### Key Components
- **Origin:** Knights of the Toxic God (from Toxoids DLC).
- **Ethics:** 
    - **Spiritualist:** Allows the "Veneration of Saints" edict, which buffs priests, and this bonus also applies to your Knights.
    - **Xenophobe:** Enables livestock slavery to solve food issues and provides pop growth.
    - **Egalitarian:** Unlocks the Parliamentary System civic for a powerful early-game unity boost.
- **Civics:**
    - **Parliamentary System:** For the initial unity rush.
    - **Environmentalist / Planet Caretakers:** Synergizes with high stability and provides resource bonuses.
- **Traits:** **Intelligent** is mandatory. **Natural Sociologists** is chosen to support the use of biological ships.

### Early Game Plan
1.  **Habitat Focus:** All development happens on your starting orbital habitat, the "Order's Keep".
2.  **Job Spam:** Build Strongholds to increase the number of available Knight job slots. Simultaneously, stack as many Squires as your housing will allow.
3.  **Housing Reduction:** Actively seek out and apply housing reduction modifiers to enable more Squires.
4.  **Edict & Tech:** Activate the "Veneration of Saints" edict. Prioritize research that unlocks building slots and technologies that provide bonuses to scientists (as these will also buff your Knights).

### Mid to Late Game Scaling
1.  **Continue Stacking:** The entire game is focused on adding more Strongholds (for Knights) and more Squires (to buff the Knights). 
2.  **Bonus Hunting:** Hunt for any tradition, technology, or edict that provides a bonus to politicians or scientists and add it to the stack.
3.  **Resource Expansion:** Your alloy consumption will be immense. You will need to expand to other planets or conquer neighbors solely to acquire more alloys to fuel your Knights.
4.  **Exponential Growth:** As the number of Squires grows, the output of each Knight skyrockets, leading to an exponential increase in your research and unity.

### Why It's Powerful
This build is broken because the Squire job scaling has no cap. Each Squire buffs the Knights, and the Knights benefit from a wide range of stacking bonuses that were likely not intended to all apply to a single job. This creates a feedback loop where your tech output quickly outpaces anything else in the game, leading to an insurmountable advantage.

- **Required DLCs:** Toxoids Species Pack (for the origin)
- **Difficulty:** Medium (Requires understanding the specific job interactions and housing mechanics)
- **Note:** This build relies on mechanics that may be considered exploits (infinite Squires, housing reduction) and could be patched in the future.

# Build: Composer of Strands - Gaia World Spam (Shroud Psionic)

## Short Description
A tall, roleplay-focused psionic build centered on making a covenant with the "Composer of Strands" shroud patron. The goal is to rush Psionic Ascension and then use the Composer's unique "Transformation" power to convert every uninhabitable planet and moon in your starting system into a perfect Gaia world. This creates a small, easily defensible, yet highly productive empire.

## Roleplay Theme
This build is designed for a "nature druids in space" fantasy. You are a spiritualist empire that worships life and fertility, using your psionic powers to spread life (represented by purple vines and Gaia worlds) throughout the stars.

## Detailed Strategy

### Core Mechanic
The entire build relies on the **Composer of Strands** shroud patron. Once you have a covenant, you unlock the **Transformation** power. Once every 10 years, this allows you to terraform any uninhabitable rock (planet or moon) in a controlled system into a small Gaia world. Combined with the Life-Seeded origin, your empire will consist entirely of perfect, 100% habitability worlds.

### Key Components
- **Origin:** **Life-Seeded**. This gives you a size 30 Gaia capital world and the Gaia Preference trait, ensuring your pops are always perfectly happy on the worlds you create.
- **Ethics:** **Spiritualist** is mandatory for Psionic Ascension.
- **Civics:** **Environmentalist** is key, as it allows you to generate unity from the "Shrouded Vegetation" blockers that the Composer adds to your worlds. **The Chosen** can also fit the theme well.
- **System Choice:** The **Sol system** is highly recommended as your starting system because it is guaranteed to have many uninhabitable rocky planets and moons to transform.

### Early Game Plan
1.  **Contact the Composer:** As soon as you breach the Shroud, choose the "Composer of Strands" as your patron.
2.  **Farm Attunement:** Assign all your envoys to "Improve Shroud Attunement" and complete "The Calling" tasks to reach 800 attunement as quickly as possible.
3.  **Stay Small:** DO NOT expand beyond your starting constellation, or even your starting system. This is a "tall" build; territory is irrelevant.

### Mid Game Transition
1.  **Psionic Rush:** Focus all efforts on rushing the Psionic Ascension path (the Mind Over Matter and Transcendence ascension perks).
2.  **Form the Covenant:** Once you have Transcendence, form the covenant with the Composer of Strands.
3.  **Unity Farming:** As the Composer adds "Shrouded Vegetation" blockers to your capital, use the Environmentalist civic to farm them for massive amounts of unity.

### Late Game
1.  **Transform Worlds:** Use the Transformation power every 10 years. Target every barren planet, frozen world, and rocky moon in your home system.
2.  **Colonize:** Colonize each new Gaia world. Because they are all perfect worlds, they will be highly productive.
3.  **Defend:** With your entire empire located in a single, highly developed system (or a small constellation), it becomes incredibly easy to defend with a powerful starbase and fleet.

### Why It's Powerful
This build trades wide territory for planet quality. A single Gaia world is often more productive than three mediocre planets. By filling a single system with multiple small Gaia worlds, you create a compact, efficient, and well-defended core that can scale surprisingly well in a single-player environment.

---

- **Required DLCs:** Utopia (for Psionics), Overlord (for Shroud Patrons)
- **Difficulty:** Medium (Requires a good understanding of how to manage the Shroud)
- **Viability:** Very strong in single-player where you have time to scale. Very weak in multiplayer, as games are often too fast-paced for this strategy to come online.

# Build: Generic Tech Rush Guide (1000 Tech by Year 30)

## Short Description
A comprehensive generalized tech rush build that can achieve ~1000 tech output by year 30. This build focuses on early game optimization and can be adapted to any playstyle. It uses Parliamentary System for early unity, research-focused traits, and specialized research buildings. The guide emphasizes proper opening moves, research prioritization, and economic balance to establish a strong tech foundation.

## Detailed Strategy

### Key Principle
This is a GENERALIZED guide applicable to any Stellaris 4.0+ game. The specific choices (civics, traits) can be swapped for equivalents based on your preferred playstyle. The goal is to understand the underlying reasons for each choice.

### Core Components
- **Civics:** Start with **Parliamentary System** for early unity from factions and **Masterful Crafters** for valuable trade from Artificers, which helps with consumer goods upkeep for scientists. 
  - *Alternatives:* Parliamentary System can be replaced with other unity-generating civics like Civil Education, Beacon of Liberty, or Genesis Guides.
- **Traits:** **Intelligent** is mandatory for the +10% researcher output. Pair this with a rapid growth trait like **Rapid Breeders**. Use negative traits like **Repugnant** (no real downside) and **Wasteful** to afford the positive ones.
- **Authority:** Democratic is recommended to boost faction unity output.

### Opening Moves (First Few Years)
1.  **Check Research:** Immediately check your research options for specialized research building technologies (e.g., Xenobiology Labs, Materials Workshop). These are a top priority.
2.  **Survey:** Survey your home system right away.
3.  **Diplomacy:** Set your diplomatic stance to Isolationist for a +10% unity boost.
4.  **Market:** Buy a lump sum of 42 minerals from the market before the price inflates.
5.  **Starbase:** Replace the Crew Quarters on your starting starbase with a Hydroponics Bay. This provides 10 food without needing a pop to work a job.
6.  **Factions:** Promote your factions to increase unity output.
7.  **Policies:** Adjust policies to satisfy faction demands, further boosting their approval and output.

### Research Priority
Your main goal is to unlock the specialized research buildings. These buildings double the output of a chosen research category on a planet. Always pick the lowest-cost technology option if your desired tech isn't available, as this allows you to cycle through options faster.

### Economic Management
- Don't build mining stations in the very early game. The minerals are better spent on your first monument and colonial districts.
- The trade value generated by Artificers (from Masterful Crafters) is crucial for affording the consumer goods needed by your scientists.
- Colonize your second planet to secure a source of basic resources like minerals and food.

### Why It Works
This build front-loads unity production without needing to build unity-specific buildings, allowing you to focus on tech. The combination of Parliamentary System, Democratic authority, and Egalitarian ethics significantly boosts faction unity gain. This unity is then used to rush the Discovery tradition tree and unlock powerful research bonuses early, creating a strong foundation for a technology-dominant game.
